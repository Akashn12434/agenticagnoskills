# PowerPoint Slide Design Guide

## Standard Presentation Layout

- Title slide: title, optional subtitle, author or organization, date.
- Content slides: one title, focused bullets, clear hierarchy, consistent spacing.
- Final slide: summary, thank you, questions, or contact details.

## Content Density

- Use 3 to 6 bullets per slide.
- Keep bullets near 5 to 12 words when possible.
- Avoid large paragraphs, excessive animation, too many colors, and tiny fonts.

## Font Guidelines

- Fonts: Arial, Calibri, Helvetica; secondary options: Verdana, Tahoma, Times New Roman.

| Layout Element | Font Size Target | Weight |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Main title | 36 to 44 pt | Bold |
| Slide title | 28 to 36 pt | Bold |
| Body bullets | 18 to 24 pt | Normal |
| Footer/source notes | 10 to 14 pt | Regular |

## Color Guidelines

- Professional Blue: Navy `#1C2833`, Slate `#2E4053`, Light Gray `#D5D8DC`.
- Modern Teal: Teal `#5EA8A7`, Dark Teal `#117A65`, White `#FFFFFF`.
- Executive Dark: Black `#000000`, Gold `#BF9A4A`, Cream `#F8F6F0`.

## Layout Rules and Recommendations

- Title + bullets: definitions, explanations, summaries.
- Two columns: comparisons, pros/cons, before/after cases.
- Three columns: categories, frameworks, feature matrices.
- Do not stack charts below dense text in one slide.
- Keep margins, alignment, and visual balance consistent.
- Use full-bleed images with high-contrast overlays only for title or impact slides.
